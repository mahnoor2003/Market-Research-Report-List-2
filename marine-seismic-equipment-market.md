<p><p>Marine seismic equipment refers to various types of instruments used in underwater seismic surveys to study the earth's structure and explore for oil and gas reserves beneath the seabed. Sub-bottom profilers are used to map the stratigraphy of the seafloor. Seismic sensors, like scalar and vector sensors, geophones, and hydrophones, detect and record seismic waves. Streamers are long cables with sensors that are towed behind survey vessels, with single or multiple streamers used depending on survey requirements. Air and water guns are used to generate controlled acoustic energy for seismic imaging.</p></p>
