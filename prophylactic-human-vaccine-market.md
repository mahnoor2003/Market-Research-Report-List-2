<p><p>The prophylactic human vaccine market can be categorized into different types based on the manufacturing process and composition. Inactivated vaccines are made by killing the disease-causing microorganisms, while attenuated vaccines use weakened forms of the pathogen. Both types stimulate an immune response without causing severe illness. Other vaccine types include subunit, toxoid, conjugate, and DNA vaccines that use specific parts of the pathogen or its toxins to trigger an immune response. These variations in vaccine types allow for a diversified approach in preventing diseases and protecting public health.</p></p>
