<p><p>Inhalation anesthetic drugs are divided into two types: general anesthesia drugs and local anesthesia drugs. General anesthesia drugs are administered via inhalation to induce complete unconsciousness and pain control during surgical procedures. They act by suppressing the central nervous system and inhibiting pain signals. On the other hand, local anesthesia drugs are used to numb a specific area of the body, preventing pain sensation during minor procedures such as dental work or suturing. They are applied topically or through injections and only affect the targeted area.</p></p>
