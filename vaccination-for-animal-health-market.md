<p><p>Vaccination for animal health encompasses various types aimed at preventing diseases in animals. Live attenuated vaccination involves using weakened forms of pathogens, promoting an immune response without causing illness. Inactivated vaccination uses killed pathogens or their components to stimulate an immune response. DNA vaccination introduces genetically engineered DNA of pathogens into the body to trigger an immune response. Other vaccination types include vectored vaccines, subunit vaccines, and recombinant vaccines, which are based on specific components of pathogens. Each method offers unique benefits and considerations, contributing to a comprehensive approach in safeguarding animal health.</p></p>
