<p><p>Food allergen testing is a crucial process to detect and quantify potential allergens present in food products, ensuring consumer safety. Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR)-based testing utilizes a DNA amplification technique to identify allergenic genes, providing highly accurate results. Immunoassay-based/ELISA testing employs specific antibodies to recognize and measure allergenic proteins. Other food allergen testing methods include mass spectrometry, which enables the identification and quantification of allergenic proteins. These testing methods assist in conforming to food label regulations, enhancing food safety, and reducing the risk of allergic reactions.</p></p>
