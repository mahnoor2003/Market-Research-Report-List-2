Cyclotrons are used in the production of radioisotopes, such as technetium-99m, which are widely used in diagnostic imaging procedures like positron emission tomography (PET). The increasing prevalence of cancer and the rising demand for accurate diagnostic imaging are expected to fuel the demand for cyclotrons.</p><p>Another trend in the cyclotron market is the development of compact and portable cyclotrons. Traditional cyclotrons are large and require significant infrastructure.
