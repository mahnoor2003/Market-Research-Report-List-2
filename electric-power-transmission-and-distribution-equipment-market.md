<p><ul><li>Overhead Transmission</li><li>Underground Transmission</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Electric power transmission and distribution equipment refer to the infrastructure required to transmit and distribute electrical energy from power plants to consumers. Overhead transmission involves the use of suspended transmission lines and towers to transport electricity over long distances. This type requires less initial investment but is susceptible to weather conditions. Underground transmission, on the other hand, involves the use of cables buried beneath the ground to transport electricity. It is more expensive to install than overhead transmission but offers advantages such as better aesthetics, lower maintenance, and reduced susceptibility to weather-related disruptions.</p></p>
