<p><p>The bio-engineered stent market consists of two main types: metal-based bio-engineered stents and polymer-based bio-engineered stents. Metal-based stents are typically made of materials like stainless steel or cobalt-chromium alloys, allowing for high strength and flexibility. Polymer-based stents, on the other hand, are made from biocompatible materials like polylactic acid or polyurethane. These stents offer advantages such as lower risk of allergic reactions and higher drug elution capacity. Both types of stents aim to provide improved cardiovascular treatment options.</p></p>
