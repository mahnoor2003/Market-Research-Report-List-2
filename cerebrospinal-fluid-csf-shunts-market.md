<p><p>Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF) shunts are medical devices used primarily to treat hydrocephalus, a condition characterized by the accumulation of excess cerebrospinal fluid within the brain. CSF shunts function by diverting the flow of fluid from areas of the brain where it accumulates to other parts of the body where it can be reabsorbed.</p><p>The global CSF shunts market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. Factors contributing to the market growth include increasing prevalence of hydrocephalus, advancements in shunt technology, rising geriatric population, and a growing awareness about neurological disorders. Additionally, the rising demand for minimally invasive procedures and the availability of reimbursement options further propel market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the adoption of new technologies such as programmable valves and magnetic shunts are driving market growth. Programmable valves provide more control over cerebrospinal fluid drainage and allow adjustment without the need for surgical intervention. Magnetic shunts incorporate magnetic components that allow medical professionals to control the opening and closing of the shunt without surgery.</p><p>However, certain challenges hinder the market growth of CSF shunts.
