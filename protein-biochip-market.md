<p><p>A protein biochip, also known as a protein microarray, is a tool used for detecting and measuring the abundance of proteins in biological samples. It consists of a solid surface, such as a glass slide or a silicon chip, onto which different types of proteins are immobilized or printed in an ordered and miniaturized format. Protein biochips enable high-throughput analysis of protein interactions, protein expression levels, and protein-protein interactions.</p><p>The protein biochip market is witnessing significant growth, driven by the increasing demand for personalized medicine, advancements in proteomic technologies, and growing applications of protein biochips in drug discovery and development. <p><strong>The Protein Biochip Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Active Chip</li><li>Passive Chip</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Protein biochips are a type of microarray used in protein analysis and diagnostics. The market for protein biochips is categorized into active and passive chips. Active chips involve immobilized proteins that engage in specific interactions with target molecules, enabling direct measurement. Passive chips, on the other hand, require additional labeling or tags to measure protein interactions. Active chips offer real-time detection and higher sensitivity, while passive chips may be more cost-effective. Both types have their advantages and limitations, making them suitable for different protein analysis applications.</p></p>
