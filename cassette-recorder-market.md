<p><p>Cassette recorder market types can be classified into two categories: Type I cassettes and Type II cassettes. Type I cassettes, also known as normal bias cassettes, are the standard and most commonly used type. They offer good sound quality and are suitable for general recording purposes. Type II cassettes, also called high bias cassettes, provide superior sound quality and are designed for high-fidelity recordings. They feature higher magnetic coercivity, allowing them to capture more detailed audio. Type II cassettes were popular among audiophiles and studios seeking professional-grade recordings during the 1980s.</p></p>
