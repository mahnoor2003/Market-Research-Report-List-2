<p><p>Organic Light-Emitting Diodes (OLEDs) are a type of display technology that emit light when an electric current is applied. The OLED market can be categorized into three types: monochrome, multi-color, and full-color. Monochrome OLEDs display images in a single color, typically white or yellow, and are commonly used in small devices like digital watches. Multi-color OLEDs can display images in multiple colors, enhancing visual quality and are commonly found in televisions and smartphones. Full-color OLEDs offer a wider range of colors, providing more vibrant and realistic images, which are commonly used in high-end displays and monitors.</p></p>
