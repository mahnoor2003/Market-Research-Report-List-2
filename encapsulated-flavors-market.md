<p><p>Encapsulated flavors refer to the process of coating or encapsulating flavor molecules to protect them from external factors like heat, moisture, or light. Various methods are employed in the market for encapsulating flavors, including fluid bed coating, spray drying, glass encapsulation, and spray chilling/congealing. Fluid bed coating involves applying a coating material onto the flavor molecules through fluidization. Spray drying utilizes a spray to convert the flavor into dried particles. Glass encapsulation encloses flavors in a glassy shell. Spray chilling/congealing freezes the flavors quickly to form small particles.</p></p>
