<p><strong>The In-situ Hybridization (ISH) Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization (FISH)</li><li>Chromogenic In Situ Hybridization</li></ul></p>
<p><p>In-situ Hybridization (ISH) is a technique used in molecular biology to study the localization and expression of specific genes within cells or tissues. There are two major types of ISH markets: </p><p>1. Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization (FISH): This method uses fluorescently-labeled DNA probes to bind to specific target sequences in the specimen, allowing for visualization under a fluorescence microscope. FISH is commonly used to detect chromosomal abnormalities and gene amplifications in cancer research.</p><p>2. Chromogenic In Situ Hybridization: This method uses enzyme-labeled DNA probes that yield a colored reaction product when bound to target sequences. Chromogenic ISH is commonly employed for analyzing gene expression patterns and protein distribution in clinical pathology and research settings.</p></p>
