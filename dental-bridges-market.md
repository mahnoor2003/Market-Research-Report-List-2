<p><p>Dental bridges are fixed dental restorations used to replace one or more missing teeth by joining an artificial tooth, known as a pontic, to adjacent teeth or dental implants. The purpose of dental bridges is to improve both the functionality and aesthetics of the mouth, restoring the ability to eat, speak, and smile confidently. They are usually made from materials like porcelain, metal alloys, or a combination of both.</p><p>The dental bridges market is witnessing significant growth and is expected to continue to do so in the future. Several factors contribute to this growth. Firstly, the increasing prevalence of dental disorders, such as tooth decay and gum diseases, is driving the demand for dental bridges. Additionally, the growing geriatric population, which often requires dental restorations due to age-related tooth loss, is also fostering market growth.</p><p>Technological advancements and innovations in dental materials are further enhancing the market's expansion. For instance, the adoption of CAD/CAM technology in the design and fabrication of dental bridges has improved precision, durability, and aesthetics.
