<p><p>The EGFR Mutation Test is a medical diagnostic test used to detect mutations in the epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR) gene. Mutations in the EGFR gene are often found in non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) patients, and they indicate a higher likelihood of response to EGFR tyrosine kinase inhibitors (TKIs). The test helps in determining the appropriate treatment for NSCLC patients by identifying those who are more likely to benefit from EGFR TKIs.</p><p>The EGFR Mutation Test Market is experiencing steady growth, driven by factors such as the increasing incidence of lung cancer, growing awareness about personalized medicine, and advancements in diagnostic technologies. Additionally, the rising demand for targeted therapies and the need for accurate and effective treatment options are also fueling market growth.</p><p>The market is also influenced by various latest trends. One of the key trends is the increasing adoption of liquid biopsy tests for EGFR mutation detection. Liquid biopsy tests provide a less invasive and real-time analysis of the tumor's genetic profile, making them a preferred choice for monitoring treatment response and disease progression.</p><p>Another trend is the integration of next-generation sequencing (NGS) technologies in EGFR Mutation Testing. NGS allows simultaneous analysis of multiple genes and provides comprehensive genetic information, enabling better understanding of disease progression and identification of potential therapeutic targets.</p><p>Furthermore, the development of companion diagnostic tests is gaining momentum in the EGFR Mutation Test Market.
