<p><p>Cathodic electrocoating, also known as electrodeposition or electrocoating, is a process used to coat metal surfaces with a durable, corrosion-resistant finish. It involves immersing the metal substrate in a tank containing an electrically charged paint emulsion. The metal surface acts as a cathode, attracting and depositing the paint particles onto its surface.</p><p>The cathodic electrocoating market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. The increasing demand for corrosion-resistant coatings in end-use industries such as automotive, appliances, and metal furniture is one of the major factors driving market growth. Cathodic electrocoating provides excellent corrosion protection and has become an essential process for enhancing the durability and performance of metal substrates in various applications.</p><p>Additionally, stringent environmental regulations and the growing focus on sustainability have propelled the adoption of cathodic electrocoating. The process is considered eco-friendly as it reduces volatile organic compounds (VOCs) and provides a more efficient utilization of coating materials.
