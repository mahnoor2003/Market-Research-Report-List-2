<p><p>Excimer Laser Annealing (ELA) is a process used in the semiconductor industry to improve the performance and quality of thin-film transistors (TFTs) on glass substrates. XeCl (308nm) ELA and KrF (248nm) ELA are two types of ELA techniques. XeCl ELA utilizes a xenon chloride laser with a wavelength of 308nm, while KrF ELA uses a krypton fluoride laser with a wavelength of 248nm. Both techniques selectively heat and crystallize the amorphous silicon film, allowing for higher TFT mobility and improved device performance. These technologies are crucial in modernizing electronic displays and other semiconductor applications.</p></p>
