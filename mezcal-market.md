Joven mezcal is unaged and has a strong smoky flavor. Reposado mezcal is aged in oak barrels for a short period of time, resulting in a smoother and more nuanced taste. Anejo mezcal is aged in oak barrels for a longer period, acquiring a richer and more complex flavor profile. These different market types offer a variety of options to cater to different taste preferences in the mezcal industry.</p></p>
