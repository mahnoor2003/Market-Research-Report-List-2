<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The silicone room temperature glue market includes two types: condensed type and addition type. Condensed type silicone glues are created by mixing a base polymer with a cross-linker, which leads to the release of a volatile byproduct. This type of glue is commonly used for general-purpose applications. Addition type silicone glues, on the other hand, do not release any byproducts during the curing process. These glues are widely utilized in industries such as electronics, medical, and automotive due to their high strength, flexibility, and resistance to extreme temperatures.</p></p>
