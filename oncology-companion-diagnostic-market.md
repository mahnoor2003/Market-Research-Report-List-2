<p><p>The Oncology Companion Diagnostic market includes various types of diagnostic technologies. Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) is a widely used technique that amplifies DNA sequences and helps identify specific mutations. Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) facilitates the analysis of genetic variations, enabling the detection of potential biomarkers. Immunohistochemistry (IHC) utilizes antibodies to identify specific proteins in tissue samples. In situ hybridization (ISH)/Fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH) are used to visualize specific DNA or RNA sequences within tissues. Lastly, there are other diagnostic technologies available in the market offering different approaches for identifying oncology biomarkers.</p></p>
