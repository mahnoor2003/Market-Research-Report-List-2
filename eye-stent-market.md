<p><p>The Eye Stent is a medical device used in ophthalmic surgery to treat glaucoma. Glaucoma is a chronic eye disease characterized by increased intraocular pressure that damages the optic nerve, leading to vision loss. The Eye Stent is a small implant made of biocompatible materials, typically titanium, and is surgically inserted into the eye's drainage system to improve the outflow of aqueous humor, thereby reducing intraocular pressure.</p><p>The Eye Stent Market has witnessed significant growth in recent years due to the rising prevalence of glaucoma and an increase in awareness about early diagnosis and treatment. <p><p>The Eye Stent market offers two types of stents: Polymer and Gelatin. Polymer stents are made of synthetic materials that are biocompatible and durable. They are designed to provide structural support to the eyes and improve fluid drainage, reducing the risk of glaucoma. On the other hand, Gelatin stents are derived from animal collagen and are biodegradable. They can be absorbed by the body over time, making them suitable for patients who may require temporary eye stents. Both types aim to alleviate intraocular pressure and promote better eye health.</p></p>
