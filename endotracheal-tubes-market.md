<p><p>Endotracheal tubes (ETTs) are medical devices used during mechanical ventilation to maintain an airway in critically ill patients, ensuring an adequate exchange of oxygen and removal of carbon dioxide. These tubes are inserted through the mouth or nose and passed into the trachea, allowing for assisted breathing.</p><p>The global endotracheal tubes market has witnessed significant growth in recent years. The increasing prevalence of respiratory diseases such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), pneumonia, and asthma necessitates the use of ETTs. Additionally, rising surgical and emergency care procedures contribute to the market's expansion, as ETTs are commonly used in anesthesia and airway management during surgeries.</p><p>Furthermore, technological advancements have led to the development of innovative ETTs with enhanced features, such as subglottic suctioning capabilities, cuff pressure monitoring devices, and antimicrobial coatings. These advancements improve patient safety and reduce the risk of complications, further driving market growth.</p><p>The market also benefits from the growing geriatric population, as older individuals are more susceptible to respiratory disorders. Moreover, increasing healthcare expenditure and infrastructure development in emerging economies contribute to the overall market growth.</p><p>However, the market faces certain challenges, including complications associated with ETTs, such as infections, ventilator-associated pneumonia, and tube displacement. These issues can hinder market growth to some extent.</p><p>Overall, the endotracheal tubes market is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 6.80% during the forecast period. The demand for ETTs is expected to rise due to the increasing prevalence of respiratory diseases, technological advancements, and the expanding healthcare sector worldwide.</p></p>

Other types of tubes include double-lumen endotracheal tubes, bioactive endotracheal tubes, and preformed endotracheal tubes.
