<p><p>A tissue microarrayer is an advanced laboratory instrument used for the production of tissue microarrays (TMAs). TMAs are widely used in research and diagnostics as a high-throughput tool for the analysis of multiple tissue specimens on a single slide. The tissue microarrayer enables the precise and efficient extraction and arrangement of tissue samples into a block, which can be sliced and placed on slides for subsequent analysis.</p><p>The tissue microarrayer market is experiencing significant growth and is expected to expand at a CAGR of 10.8% during the forecast period. Fully automated microarrayers are equipped with advanced robotics and software, allowing for high precision and efficiency in sample spotting onto slides. Semi-automated microarrayers involve a combination of manual and automated processes, requiring some human intervention. Manual microarrayers rely solely on manual spotting techniques, which can be time-consuming and less accurate.
