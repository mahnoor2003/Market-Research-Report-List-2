<p><p>Cell-based humanized mouse models are used in biomedical research to simulate human immune responses in a living organism. CD34 humanized mouse models involve engrafting human CD34+ hematopoietic stem cells into immunocompromised mice, which leads to the development of a functional human immune system. PBMC humanized mouse models are created by injecting human peripheral blood mononuclear cells into mice, allowing the study of specific human immune cell populations. BLT humanized mouse models involve implanting human fetal liver, thymus, and hematopoietic stem cells into mice, resulting in a more complete human immune system model.</p></p>
