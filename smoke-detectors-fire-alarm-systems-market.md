<p><p>Smoke detectors are devices that detect smoke in the environment and are primarily used to alert people in case of fire emergencies. They can be categorized into two types: Ionization smoke detectors, which use a small amount of radioactive material to detect smoke particles, and photoelectric smoke detectors, which use a beam of light to detect smoke. Fire alarm systems, on the other hand, are comprehensive systems designed to detect fires, alert occupants, and trigger appropriate responses like activating sprinklers or notifying emergency services. These systems may include smoke detectors, heat detectors, and alarm bells or sirens.</p></p>
