<p><p>Follicular lymphoma drugs are medications that are specifically used for the treatment of follicular lymphoma, which is a type of non-Hodgkin lymphoma. Follicular lymphoma is a slow-growing cancer that develops from the white blood cells known as lymphocytes. It is characterized by the presence of abnormal B lymphocytes in lymph nodes and bone marrow.</p><p>There are various types of drugs available for the treatment of follicular lymphoma, including chemotherapy drugs, immunotherapy drugs, targeted therapies, and monoclonal antibodies. Chemotherapy drugs such as bendamustine and cyclophosphamide are commonly used to kill cancer cells. Immunotherapy drugs like rituximab help in stimulating the immune system to recognize and destroy cancer cells. Targeted therapies, such as B-cell receptor inhibitors, aim at blocking specific proteins that promote cancer cell growth. Monoclonal antibodies like obinutuzumab and ofatumumab also help in targeting and destroying cancer cells.</p><p>The follicular lymphoma drugs market is projected to experience significant growth in the coming years.
