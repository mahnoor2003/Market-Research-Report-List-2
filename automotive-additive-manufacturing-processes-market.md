<p><ul><li>Powder Bed Fusion</li><li>Binder Jetting</li><li>Directed Energy Deposition</li><li>Material Extrusion</li><li>Material Jetting</li><li>Sheet Lamination</li><li>Vat Polymerization</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Automotive additive manufacturing processes refer to the various methods used to create three-dimensional objects in the automotive industry. These processes include Powder Bed Fusion, Binder Jetting, Directed Energy Deposition, Material Extrusion, Material Jetting, Sheet Lamination, and Vat Polymerization. Powder Bed Fusion uses a laser or electron beam to melt and fuse powdered material together. Binder Jetting involves depositing a liquid binding agent onto powdered material layers to create solid objects. Directed Energy Deposition utilizes a nozzle to deposit melted material onto a substrate. Material Extrusion involves the layer-by-layer deposition of melted material through a nozzle. Material Jetting uses printheads to deposit droplets of liquid material onto a build platform. Sheet Lamination involves layering and bonding sheets of material together. Vat Polymerization employs a light source to selectively cure liquid resin into solid shapes.</p></p>
