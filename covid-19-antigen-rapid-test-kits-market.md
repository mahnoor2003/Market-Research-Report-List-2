Nasopharyngeal swab involves taking a sample from the back of the nasal passage, while nasal swab collects a sample from the nostrils. Oropharyngeal swab collects a sample from the back of the throat, and saliva swab uses saliva as the specimen. These different methods allow for testing COVID-19 infection using alternative ways of collecting samples.</p></p>
