<p><p>In the high availability server market, different levels of availability are offered to meet the varying needs of businesses. Availability Level 1 typically refers to basic redundancy measures, such as duplicate components or power supply. Level 2 offers fault tolerance with redundant systems and automatic failover capabilities. Level 3 provides higher reliability through proactive measures, such as predictive maintenance or system monitoring. Level 4 ensures continuous availability by incorporating advanced technologies like clustering or load balancing. Level 5 represents the highest level of availability with non-stop operations, often achieved through advanced fault tolerance and real-time failover mechanisms.</p></p>
