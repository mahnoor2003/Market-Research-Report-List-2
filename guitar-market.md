<p><p>The guitar market can be divided into two main types: acoustic guitars and electric guitars. Acoustic guitars are primarily made of wood and produce sound through the vibration of their strings, without the need for amplification. They are popular for their natural and warm tone, and are commonly used in folk, country, and classical music. Electric guitars, on the other hand, require amplification to produce sound. They feature pickups that convert the vibration of the strings into electric signals, which are then amplified through an amplifier. Electric guitars are prominent in genres like rock, blues, and jazz due to their versatility and ability to produce various tones through effects and amplification.</p></p>
